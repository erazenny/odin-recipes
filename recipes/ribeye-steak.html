Best Ribeye Steak recipe for date night

Go back home.

Description

Sink your teeth into a perfectly marbled ribeye steak, seared to a caramelized crust and bursting with rich, beefy flavor.
This thick-cut masterpiece boasts a tender, buttery texture that melts in your mouth with every bite.
Expertly seasoned with a hint of coarse salt, cracked pepper, and finished with a touch of garlic-herb butter,
it delivers a bold, savory taste that satisfies to the last bite. Whether grilled or pan-seared, this ribeye is the ultimate
indulgence for true steak lovers.


Ingredients

  • 15oz Ribeye steak
  • 1 Tablespoon of Himalayan Salt
  • 1 Tablespoon of Ground Black Pepper
  • Olive Oil
  • Butter
  • Garlic Cloves
  • Rosemary and Thyme
  • Time and Patience

Steps

  1. An hour before cooking your steak, cover it with oil and season with salt (LIGHTLY) and pepper
  2. Put the steak in the fridge to get the moisture absorbed
  3. After an hour, take the steak out and let rest for 30 minutes
  4. Get a cast iron skillet (it doesn't have to be a cast iron but it works), put it on the stove, and turn the heat to high.
    (To make sure the pan in ready, pour a few droplets of water to the pan; if it beads around that means the steak is ready to sear)
  5. Add oil to the pan and then place the ribeye onto the pan
  6. After 3 minutes, flip sides
  7. After two minutes, take the pan off the heat and add your aromatics (butter, thyme, rosemary, garlic)
  8. Lean the pan a bit to the side, grab a spoon and start scooping the button and pouring on the steak (repeatedly)
  9. After a minute, flip and repeat the basting process
  10. Take your ribeye off the pan and put on a cutting board (wooded preferably)
  11. OPTIONAL: if you have the patience, let rest for about 5-7 minutes for the juices to really hold in
